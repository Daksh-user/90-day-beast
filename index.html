Knowledge is Power
Pro Tips & Rules
The rules that separate people who finish from people who quit.
Rule 01
Never Miss Monday
Starting the week strong sets your momentum for every day after. Missing Monday is the first domino that knocks everything else over. No exceptions.
Rule 02
Sleep 7–9 Hours
Muscle is built during sleep, not during training. If you're skipping sleep, you're throwing away half your results. Prioritize sleep like a second workout.
Rule 03
Progress Over Perfection
A bad workout is infinitely better than no workout. Showing up on your worst day is what builds discipline. Perfection is the enemy of consistency.
Rule 04
Track Everything
Take weekly photos. Measure your waist. Log your reps. Numbers don't lie. When you feel like quitting, your data will show you how far you've come.
Rule 05
Warm Up Every Time
5–10 minutes of dynamic warm-up before every session. Injuries are the #1 reason people quit. One torn muscle can set you back months. Protect yourself.
Rule 06
Drink 3L of Water Daily
Dehydration kills performance, recovery, and focus. Before you think you're hungry, drink water. Before every workout, drink water. Keep a bottle with you always.
Rule 07
Form Before Weight
10 perfect reps beats 20 sloppy ones every single time. Bad form means injury and zero muscle activation. Slow down, feel the muscle, earn the movement.
Rule 08
Rest Days Are Sacred
Active recovery on rest days — walk, stretch, foam roll. Your muscles grow during rest, not during training. Killing yourself 7 days a week leads to burnout.
Rule 09
Protein Every Meal
Hit 1.6–2g of protein per kg of bodyweight every day. Protein feeds your muscles, keeps you full, and prevents muscle loss while cutting. No exceptions.
Rule 10
Day 1 to Day 90
The hardest part is Day 1 and the days right before quitting. Once you get past Day 30, momentum kicks in. Trust the process. The results are waiting at the finish line.
